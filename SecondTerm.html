My Strategies

Speaking

Reading aloud

Choose a paragraph, a conversation, or a part of a book you like and read it aloud. Try to pay attention to your pronunciation, pauses (based on the punctuation) and intonation.
📎 Evidence required Recordings, videos and a reflection.
Speaking

Self-recording

Choose a topic appropriate for your level and talk about it for a few minutes. Record yourself as naturally as possible. Then listen back and identify weak areas to improve.
📎 Evidence required Recordings or videos of your self-recording session, and a reflection identifying what you found and what you will improve.
Writing

Personalizing

Find explanations and examples of a particular grammar form, then write your own sentences, examples, or a short composition using that grammar structure in a personal and meaningful way.
📎 Evidence required Written sentences or paragraphs demonstrating the grammar topic, and a reflection about your learning process.

I have used the future tense forms (will, be going to, and would)

-I am going to improve my English during this semester.
-I am going to practice speaking more in my classes.
-Next semester, I am meeting my academic advisor to discuss my progress.
-My classmates and I are preparing a software project for next month.
-I am visiting the university library tomorrow to study programming.
-If I had more free time, I would study English every day.
-If I spoke English better, I would feel more confident in presentations.
-If I finished my studies successfully, I would apply for a technology company.
-I am going to continue learning new programming languages.
-My teachers are reviewing our final projects next week.

Writing

Literature for Grammar

Read a short text (poem, essay, or story appropriate for your level). Analyze its grammar structures, imitate the style, and create your own original writing based on what you read.
📎 Evidence required Your original writing imitating the text's style, and a reflection about the grammar structures you practiced.
